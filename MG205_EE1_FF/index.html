Question

Who should be a CEO of a family firm?

Focus

  • Shareholders.
  • Societyâ€™s impact through producitivity.

Theoretical predictions

Choose a family member

  1. Direct knowledge transfer from previous CEO to incoming family-member CEO.
  2. Culture of the company, and know-how about industry.
  3. Family is part of firmâ€™s identity, hence, the employees could retain the attachment to the company.
  4. Family member is emotionally invested in the firm.

Theoretical predictions

Choose an external candidate

  1. External candidate could be more talented. (Larger pool implies higher competition)
  2. Career incentives and prove their worth earlier.
  3. Lack of attachment makes tough decisions easier to take.

Data

  • Data set: COMPUSTAT
  • Object of study: Non-financial and non-utility firms in 1994.
  • Sample restrictions:
    • Found prior to 1987.
    • Transition ocurred between 1980 and 2001.
    • Sample size: 335 firms.
  • Pedigree of CEO? Incoming CEO attended a US selective college.

Measurement of CEOâ€™s performance

Abnormal returns

\[ \begin{align} \text{Abnormal return}_{it+2} &= \underbrace{\frac{\text{Share price}_{it+2}-\text{Share price}_{it}}{\text{Share price}_{it}}}_{\text{Firm return}} \\ &- \underbrace{\frac{\text{Market index}_{it+2}-\text{Market index}_{it}}{\text{Market index}_{it}}}_{\text{Market return}} \end{align} \]

Measurement of CEOâ€™s performance

Income-to-assets ratio

Operating income of a firm.

\[ \begin{align} \Delta\text{Profits}_{it} &= \frac{\text{ItA Ratio}_{it+1}+\text{ItA Ratio}_{it+2}+\text{ItA Ratio}_{it+3}}{3} \\ &-\frac{\text{ItA Ratio}_{it-1}+\text{ItA Ratio}_{it-2}+\text{ItA Ratio}_{it-3}}{3} \end{align} \] \(\Delta\text{Profits}_{it}\) is the (average over three years) return on assets following the incoming CEO joining firm \(i\) minus the (average over three years) return on assets prior to the incoming CEO joining firm \(i\).

Hypotheses

  • Nepotism leads to lower firm performance.
  • Family transitions to lead to worse performance (relative to unrelated transitions) especially when the family candidate did not attend a selective college.
